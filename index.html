It's Time to Shatter the Glass Ceiling

It is 2017, and women are still not being treated equally to men, from the wage gap and microagressions in the workplace to policies that burden working mothers. Look no further than Susan J. Fowler's account on her experience at Uber to see this in action. We all have a role to call out sexism, and to support companies that are doing well with treating their female employees. That's where ActiveRank comes in. This extension enables shoppers and internet users to see how women-friendly the companies they shop at or support are, based on crowd-sourced reviews and metrics from women working at the companies.

Features

Company ratings

Find out how the companies you shop at treat their female employees.

Breakdowns

Obviously, companies can have the same ranking but have extremely different work conditions for women. See a breakdown of ratings, to see how the company does in benefits, salary, and representation of women.

Suggestions

Find out which companies that are more women-friendly are selling what you're buying.

Our Methodology

We use a weighted algorithm using self-reported salary reviews from women on Payscale, the leading source of workplace reviews, and FairyGodBoss, which is a leading source for benefits data. Due to lack of EEOC data, it is extremely challenging to query data fromt thoe sources. We are always looking for suggestions to better ways to rank, so if you have any suggestions, shoot them over to activerankapp@gmail.com!
